Breast Care Center — Screening, Diagnosis, Surgery & Survivorship

End-to-end breast health services—from risk assessment and screening to advanced surgery, systemic therapy and long-term follow-up. We focus on early detection, breast-conserving options, oncoplastic outcomes, and compassionate counselling for you and your family.

Concerns We Manage

  • Breast lump, pain, nipple discharge or inversion, skin dimpling
  • Abnormal screening mammogram or ultrasound findings
  • Benign conditions: fibroadenoma, cysts, mastitis/abscess
  • High-risk individuals (family history, BRCA, prior chest radiation)
  • Breast cancer: DCIS, early & locally advanced, metastatic disease

Screening & Diagnostics

  • Mammography (age-appropriate) and targeted breast ultrasound
  • Breast MRI where indicated (dense breasts, high-risk, problem solving)
  • Image-guided biopsies: core-needle, vacuum-assisted, FNAC as advised
  • Pathology with receptor testing (ER/PR), HER2, Ki-67; genomic assays when appropriate
  • Staging: chest/abdomen imaging, bone scan or PET-CT (case-based)

Surgery & Reconstruction

  • Breast-conserving surgery (lumpectomy) with clear margin strategy
  • Modified radical mastectomy where required
  • Sentinel lymph node biopsy; axillary dissection when indicated
  • Oncoplastic techniques for shape symmetry and better cosmetic outcomes
  • Immediate or delayed reconstruction (implant or autologous; case-based planning)

Systemic & Radiation Therapy (Coordinated Care)

  • Chemotherapy day-care with protocol-driven regimens
  • Hormonal therapy for ER/PR-positive disease
  • Targeted therapy (e.g., anti-HER2) and immunotherapy where eligible
  • Adjuvant or palliative radiation therapy via partner centers as required

Support, Rehab & Survivorship

  • Pain control, anti-emetic and infection-prevention protocols
  • Lymphedema prevention & management, physiotherapy and shoulder rehab
  • Nutritional counselling, fertility discussion (where relevant), psychosocial support
  • Survivorship plan: follow-up schedule, self-exam education, lifestyle guidance

Red Flags — Visit Early

  • New lump, bloody nipple discharge, nipple/skin changes (peau d’orange)
  • Rapidly growing lump, persistent pain, non-healing infection/abscess
  • Breathlessness, bone pain, weight loss in known breast cancer

Why Choose Pulse Hospital

  • Team-based breast care with surgery, medical oncology and imaging pathways
  • Oncoplastic focus for function and aesthetics
  • Personalised plans via tumor board discussion when needed
  • 24×7 support, transparent counselling, and insurance/TPA assistance
